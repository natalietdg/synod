# Synod — 3-minute demo script (final cut)

The website is the paper. The video is the trailer. Its job: make a judge
think "I need five more minutes with this one."

Record the live site (http://47.84.225.84) at 1440×900, cursor visible.
VO ≈ 350 words — the silences are scripted, don't fill them. Jump-cut all
waiting. Rehearse once off-camera so live results are cached (instant on
camera, honestly labeled as recorded).

The aha moment is engineered for ~1:05: **the chair overrules the
majority because it values information over commitment.** Everything
before it builds to that; everything after it is evidence.

---

## 1 · 0:00–0:20 · HOOK — start with the loss

**SCREEN:** Cold open on §01's ledger. Cursor rests on $0 first.
Speak slower than everything else. "All ten runs" is on screen — don't
say it.

**VO:** One AI walks away from a deal worth three thousand dollars.
*(beat)* Five independent judges stop, verify the claim, and close it
instead. Same model. Same opponent. The only difference is how the
decision gets made.

## 2 · 0:20–0:40 · THE IDEA

**SCREEN:** Page top. "Agent societies argue to agreement." crossfades to
gold: "Synod bets that's the flaw." Then the pipeline, pausing on
"Judges never converge. Only the chair commits."

**VO:** Most multi-agent systems let agents debate until they agree.
Synod doesn't. *(beat — let the gold line sit)* Five judges stay
independent. Nobody persuades anybody. A deterministic chair — code, not
rhetoric — commits.

## 3 · 0:40–1:45 · THE LIVE COUNCIL — the movie

**SCREEN + VO interleaved. The silences are the point.**

- Click **▶ Run a live decision**. Jump-cut to the proceedings.
- **VO:** This is live on Qwen.
- The split renders — five voices, in character. *(2s silence)*
- **VO:** The room splits. *(2s — let them read a voice or two)*
  Four different recommendations.
- Scroll to the gold chair block. *(2s silence — let it land)*
- **VO:** Now watch the chair. *(beat)* It doesn't care who argued
  best. *(the terrain factors are on screen)* It reads uncertainty,
  exposure, and the value of learning more. *(beat)* **And it overrules
  the room.** ← the aha, ~1:05
- The outcome: probe ordered, bluff exposed. *(2s)*
- **VO:** The bluff collapses. The war never starts.
- Open the argument fold, one beat on the recorded concession.
- **VO:** A judge can change its own mind after a challenge — nobody can
  persuade the chair. The decision is arithmetic. Signed, and replayable.

## 4 · 1:45–2:10 · REMOVE A JUDGE

**SCREEN:** The nudge appears. Click Sun Tzu off. Run again (jump-cut).
The number drops. Two-second flash of the switch-matrix.

**VO:** These agents aren't decorative. Remove the one that buys
information — the council commits before checking, and the win
evaporates. *(beat)* Against a different opponent, a different judge
becomes decisive. So you keep all five.

## 5 · 2:10–2:40 · THE EVIDENCE — independence, not difficulty

**SCREEN:** §04 steady scroll: A's bars (2s) → B's ablation (2s) → C's
heatmap (2s) → "the ladder ends outside our control" → league card →
STOP on Exhibit G. Hold the 90.4% vs 88.6% cards five full seconds.

**VO:** Then we kept testing the idea against things we didn't control.
Hold-out worlds written by a rival lab's model. Published negotiation
agents. The real ANAC league. *(beat)* And finally — four thousand two
hundred seventy-seven real human negotiations. Sellers who accepted
against Synod's dissent got less.

## 6 · 2:40–3:00 · CLOSE — end on the thesis, not the implementation

**SCREEN:** §06 diagram (3s) → the ✓/✗ boundary, linger on the ✗ (3s) →
footer, hold on the GitHub URL through the final line.

**VO:** Built on Qwen. The models reason. Pure code commits. Every
decision is signed and reproducible. *(beat)* One claim remains
unearned: live human deployment. *(beat)* Most agent societies divide
labor. **Synod divides judgment.**

## What's deliberately NOT in the video
- the debate literature (on screen in beat 2 — never read)
- §02's mechanics (beat 3 teaches it by showing)
- exhibits D, E, F; the business section; the MCP walkthrough
- the failure-mode sentence — the hook already taught it
The page carries all of it for the judge who comes back.

## Rubric anchors (85% product / 15% presentation)
- Innovation: beats 2–3 (the thesis, then the thesis running)
- Technical depth on Qwen: beat 3 (live), beat 6 (determinism, receipts)
- Problem value: beat 1 (the money)
- Measurable gain + causality (Track 3's ask): beats 1, 4, 5

  the edit, it's probably right.
