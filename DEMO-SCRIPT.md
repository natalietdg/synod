# Synod — demo video script (target 4:00)

> Setup before recording: `LLM_PROVIDER=qwen` (turbo tiers are fine), browser at
> localhost:4173, window 1440px, speed at 1× for the first round and 2× after.
> Scenario preselected: **Deceptive — adversarial RFP, BATNA bluffer**.
> Record one clean human-mode session in advance as B-roll in case the live one
> goes long. Do NOT show ARCHITECTURE.md in the video — the spine narrates it.

---

## 0:00–0:25 — Cold open (masthead only, no clicks)

> "Most agent-society projects divide *labor* — a planner, a coder, a reviewer,
> passing work down a pipeline. Synod divides **judgment**. Five AI advisors with
> genuinely different worldviews negotiate the same deal, disagree on record, and a
> neutral arbiter decides whose judgment matters *this round*. The claim is on the
> screen: against a deceptive counterparty, a strong single agent walks away with
> zero, every run. The council closes three thousand dollars. Every run. Let me show
> you why."

*(Cursor sweeps across the three stat chips. Click **Convene the council**.)*

## 0:25–1:40 — One round, narrated slowly (this IS the architecture section)

*(As the pipeline rail lights up, narrate each stage in one breath — never name a
file, never show a diagram. The rail is the diagram.)*

> "Watch the rail at the top — that's the whole architecture, live. An offer comes
> in. The **belief** updates — Bayesian, pure code, and it only reads *behavior*,
> never words. The **Intent** lens models why they're acting this way and broadcasts
> its read. Then five worldviews score every possible move **in parallel** — gain
> now, long game, intent, information, survival. They disagree — that's the point —
> so the two most opposed file formal briefs against each other…"

*(Sealed briefs open. Pause on them.)*

> "…and this dialogue has *teeth*: a defender who's genuinely persuaded gives ground,
> on record, before the decision. Then the **Arbiter** — which deliberately never
> reads their arguments, only the terrain — sets each lens's influence, and a
> **deterministic engine**, not an LLM, computes the verdict."

*(Beams fire.)*

> "Those beams are the actual votes converging — thick means agreed, faint means
> dissented. LLMs reason; code decides. And every round is **cryptographically
> receipted** —" *(click `receipt ✓ signed`, let the JSON flash open and closed)* "—
> a filed proceeding, not a chat log."

## 1:40–2:15 — The thesis moment (switch speed to 2×)

*(Rounds 2–3 stream. The moment the gold flash hits:)*

> "Round two: the counterparty claims competitor leverage — a classic bluff. The
> Learning lens prices what knowing the truth is worth, the council probes — and
> there it is: **DECEPTION DISARMED**. The bluff breaks. A single-stance agent reads
> that leverage at face value, pushes harder, and loses the deal. The council paid
> one move to learn the truth."

*(Rounds file into strips.)*

> "Notice finished rounds file themselves into one line — them, our read, the vote,
> what we sent. The whole case stays on one screen."

## 2:15–2:35 — Case closed

*(Disposition band: stamp slams, redaction slides.)*

> "Case closed: three thousand dollars of surplus, the dossier declassified — and
> the line that matters: the single-agent baseline on this same counterparty,
> same dice: **zero dollars, zero deals.**"

## 2:35–3:15 — The judge becomes the evidence (human mode)

*(Select "YOU play the counterparty", convene, type fast — or cut to the pre-recorded
session.)*

> "But you don't have to take the benchmark's word for it — **you can be the
> counterparty.** I get a secret brief: my real ceiling, a competing quote. I'm going
> to bluff…" *(type a lowball + bluff message, send; belief bar moves)* "…and watch
> the belief bar — it's reading my *behavior*, not my words. I hold firm, it suspects
> me. It probes **me** —" *(probe dock pulses)* "— hold the bluff or come clean? An
> Anthropic model played this seat with a textbook bluffing strategy and moved this
> council a hundred and two dollars in three rounds."

## 3:15–3:45 — The evidence stack (scroll, don't dwell)

*(Scroll through Exhibits A and B steadily.)*

> "Everything here is measured, seeded, and reproducible. Exhibit A: ten runs per
> type, mean and spread. Exhibit B removes one component at a time — and we publish
> the honest nulls, including the parts whose removal costs nothing. The README goes
> further: five hold-out worlds **authored adversarially by a different AI vendor**
> — the single agent walks to zero in three of them; the council closes all fifty
> runs — plus the belief layer's confusion matrix. We publish what the harness
> returns, not what flatters the architecture."

## 3:45–4:00 — The gavel close

*(Open ⚖ Hold the gavel on the last round, drag one slider to an extreme; the verdict
flips live.)*

> "And because the engine is deterministic, you can hold the gavel yourself —
> re-weight the council and the verdict answers in real time. It's also an MCP
> server, so any agent can convene this council as a tool. Most agent societies
> divide labor. **This one divides judgment** — and shows its receipts."

*(End on the masthead.)*

## Q&A pocket answers (keep these loaded)

- **"Why isn't it just the Learning lens?"** → "On our original suite they tie —
  published in Exhibit B. On five adversarial hold-out worlds we didn't author,
  the council beats it and is the only arm that's never catastrophic. You only know
  the right lens in hindsight; the council is how you don't have to know."
- **"Your belief misread a human player."** → "It read their *behavior* — they
  fabricated leverage and stonewalled, which **is** deceptive conduct regardless of
  the brief. And it played that behavior optimally: it captured their entire
  ceiling. Beliefs here are instrumental: 10/10 detecting deception, and its only
  confusions are between types whose best response is the same."
- **"EVI says ≤ cost but it probed."** → "Deterministic mode hard-gates on EVI —
  run the CLI and you'll see probes refused. Live mode treats EVI as an advisory
  referee, and every override is named on screen and signed into the receipt, same
  as the Quant. Worldviews may overrule arithmetic; they may not hide it."
- **"Who authored the opponent?"** → "Three opponent classes: our scripted GM, a
  live Qwen adversary nobody scripted, and human judges — including a rival lab's
  model. The result pattern holds across all three."
- **"Where's Qwen?"** → "Every reasoning surface — five lenses, intent, arbiter,
  the adversary — through native function calling, two model tiers for cost, and
  the council itself is consumable by a Qwen agent over MCP."
