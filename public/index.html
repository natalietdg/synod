The problem

A bluff beats a single mind.

A deceptive buyer claims three competing offers and a hard budget cap. A single-stance agent takes it at face value, pushes harder, and the deal dies — $0. It never thought to check.

The idea

Five worldviews. One judgment.

Synod fields five negotiators, each owning one question the others ignore. A doctrine-free Arbiter weighs them by the terrain — never by who argued best — then a deterministic engine decides when to trust, probe, concede, or walk.
